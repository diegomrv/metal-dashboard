## Design Context

### Users
Serious lifters who track workouts with Hevy Pro. They care about their data and want to see training trends, progression, and volume breakdowns. Technical enough to get an API key, likely familiar with fitness tracking apps. Context: reviewing training data at home or between sets.

### Brand Personality
**Polished, confident, functional.** Metal Dashboard should feel like a premium fitness product -- the kind of tool that earns trust through quality, not flashiness. Three words: **refined, data-forward, reliable.**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **References:** Strong.app (clean workout tracker, premium feel, good data presentation) + Linear.app (tight UI, fast, developer-grade polish)
- **Anti-references:** Generic dashboard templates, overly playful fitness apps, AI slop (cyan-on-dark, gradient text, glassmorphism)
- **Theme:** Light and dark mode (mauve-based oklch palette already established)
- **Typography:** IBM Plex Sans (body) + Merriweather (headings) -- already in place, keep it
- **Color:** Mauve neutrals with red/coral chart accents -- warm but not aggressive
- **Radius:** 0.45rem -- subtle rounding, not bubbly

### Design Principles
1. **Data speaks first** -- every visual element should serve the data. No decorative fluff.
2. **Tight and intentional** -- spacing, alignment, and hierarchy should feel deliberate, like Linear. Nothing loose or accidental.
3. **Premium restraint** -- polish comes from precision, not ornamentation. Let typography and whitespace do the work.
4. **Fast and responsive** -- interactions should feel instant. Optimistic UI, smooth transitions, no loading spinners where avoidable.
5. **Gym-proof** -- works on phone screens between sets. Touch targets, readable at a glance, no tiny text.
